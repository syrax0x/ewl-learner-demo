What are online English classes?
+
Online English classes are live lessons that happen over video with a real teacher. They are not pre-recorded videos or an app you work through on your own. You join from wherever you are on a platform you already know, usually Zoom or Google Meet. During the lesson, your teacher shares materials on screen and helps you practice speaking in real time. You get feedback and corrections as you go, which is something self-study just cannot give you. After each lesson, your teacher sets you homework so you keep making progress between classes. It feels like a real classroom, with the freedom of learning from anywhere.
Are your classes private or group?
+
We offer both private and group classes, so you can pick whatever suits how you like to learn. Private lessons are just you and your teacher, which means the whole hour is built around your goals and your pace. Group classes are kept small, so you still get plenty of speaking time and personal attention from the teacher. Some groups are open, which means you can join an existing class with other learners at your level. Others are closed, like a team from one company or a few friends who want to learn together. If you are not sure which format is right for you, we can help you decide on your free assessment call.
Do I learn with a real teacher or self-study material?
+
You learn with a real teacher. Every lesson is led by a qualified teacher who guides you, corrects you, and keeps you moving toward your goals. The self-study side is there to support your lessons, not to replace them. After each class, your teacher gives you homework to practice what you covered. This includes a mix of listening, reading, writing, and speaking exercises, so you build all your skills and not just one. The two work best together: your teacher does the teaching, and the homework helps it stick between classes.
Which is the best way to learn English online?
+
The best way to learn English online is with regular, live speaking practice and a real teacher who gives you feedback. Language is a skill, and like any skill, you build it by using it and by being corrected as you go. That is why live lessons work so well: you speak, you make mistakes, and your teacher helps you fix them on the spot. Consistency matters just as much as method, so a steady rhythm of classes beats occasional bursts of study. This is exactly how we teach: real teachers, real conversation, steady practice, and a path built around what you actually need.
What's your teaching approach / methodology?
+
Our teachers are CELTA-certified, which means they are trained to a recognized international standard. We follow a communicative approach, so lessons are built around using English in real situations, not just memorizing rules. You still get the grammar and vocabulary you need, but always in a way you can actually use when you speak. We make sure you build all four skills: speaking, listening, reading, and writing. Your progress is tracked through our platform, so you can see how far you have come and what to work on next. We also map out your goals from the start, so you know how and when you will reach them. The result is a clear path forward, taught by people who know how to get you there.
Do you teach British or American English?
+
We teach both British and American English, so you can learn whichever one fits your goals. If you are working with a British company or preparing for an exam like IELTS, we can focus on British English. If you are dealing with American clients or studying for a US-based test, we can focus on American English. We have teaching materials and resources for both, so the difference is built into your lessons, not just an afterthought. We also have both British and American teachers, which really helps if you want to work on your pronunciation and accent. And if you are not sure which one you need, that is completely fine, since most everyday English works perfectly well either way. We will help you decide what makes the most sense for you.
What age groups do you teach?
+
We teach children, teens, and adults, so there is a place for most learners with us. Our youngest learners are around 7 years old, and we work all the way up to adult professionals. For younger learners, lessons are kept fun and engaging while still building real skills. For teens, we often focus on school support, confidence, and exam preparation. For adults, it might be career goals, standardized tests, relocation, or simply speaking more fluently. Whatever the age, we match the teacher and the materials to the learner, so the lessons always feel right for them.
What English levels do you teach?
+
We teach all levels from A1 to C1 on the CEFR scale, which runs from absolute beginner up to advanced. If you are just starting out and barely know any English, that is completely fine, since we welcome absolute beginners. If you already speak fairly well, we can take you further and smooth out the gaps. We use the CEFR levels (A1, A2, B1, B2, C1) so your progress is clear and measured against a recognized standard. Not sure what your level is? That is exactly what the free assessment call is for, since we will check it for you before you start. For exam learners, IELTS preparation usually works best from B1 to C1, once you have a solid base to build on.
What can I focus on in my lessons?
+
What you focus on depends a little on the type of class you choose. In private lessons, you can focus on whatever matters most to you, since the whole lesson is built around your goals. People come to us for all sorts of reasons: general English, speaking with more confidence, exam preparation, interview practice, and plenty more. You can even mix focuses, so you might work mostly on speaking while also preparing for a job interview. Group classes follow a more set program, so everyone in the class moves through the same material together, but you still get to practice and grow in every lesson. One thing we are especially known for is helping learners overcome language anxiety. If speaking English makes you nervous, we will help you build real confidence step by step, in a way that feels safe and encouraging.

Can you prepare me for IELTS, TOEFL, or SAT?
+

TOEFL and SAT preparation are taught in private lessons, so the lessons are fully focused on your target score and your timeline. Alongside your lessons, you also get practice materials, mock exams, and feedback from your teacher, so you walk in knowing exactly what to expect. We build your plan around your exam date, so every lesson moves you closer to the score you need.

If you are very busy, 1x per week works too, but you will see the best progress if you keep up with your homework on our platform between lessons. Group classes usually run 2x per week, which keeps everyone moving forward together. For corporate programs, we customize the schedule around the company's goals and the team's availability. The right number really depends on your goals, and we will help you find a rhythm that fits your life.
How many lessons does it take to move up a level?
+
The honest answer is that it depends, since everyone learns at their own pace. How fast you move up depends on your starting point, how often you have lessons, and how much you practice between them. As a general guide, moving up one CEFR level usually takes around 150 to 200 guided hours of study. The lower levels often move a little faster, so A1 to A2 and A2 to B1 tend to sit near that range. The higher levels take more work, so B1 to B2 and B2 to C1 can take 200 hours or more, because the gap between levels gets wider as you advance. In our experience, learners who come consistently and keep up with their homework move through these stages noticeably faster. So the best thing you can do is stay regular with your lessons and practice, and we will help you get there as efficiently as possible.

What if I need to cancel or reschedule a lesson?
+
We understand that life happens, so you can cancel or reschedule when you need to. If you let us know at least 24 hours before your lesson, there is no charge, and you can ask to move it to another time. If you cancel less than 24 hours before, the lesson is counted as used.
What happens if I miss a lesson?
+
If you miss a lesson with no notice, we count it as a completed lesson and take it from your account. This is the same as a late cancellation, since your teacher had that time reserved for you. If you cancel in advance, at least 24 hours before, you do not lose the lesson. So the easiest way to avoid losing a lesson is to let us know as early as you can.
